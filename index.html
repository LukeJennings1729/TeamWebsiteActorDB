About Me

Hello! My name is Megan Hale and I'm a blind college student at SUNY Brockport.
I major in Adapted Physical Eductation, an avid disability in athletics awareness activist, and compete in ironman triathalons!

Picture of me, my dog Hero, and mobility specialist Samantha Gartland running tests for orienteering software developed my Microsoft, aimed at assisting blind athletes.

I first started my Ironman Triathalon journey back in high school after attending Camp Abilities Saratoga, an atheltic camp for visually impaired teens. After a week, I got the confidence to join the Avrill Park track team. After which, from high school to my time at Hudson Valley Community College, I would do running and throwing events such as discus and shot put .

I have a condition called Leber Congenital Amaurosis, or LCA, a rare type of inherited eye disorder that causes blindness at birth. This results in me having no peripheral vision and having a vision score of about 20/400. This obviously makes doing certain tasks difficult, but not impossible. For example, for my 16th birthday I got Hero, a specially trained guide dog by the non-profit Guiding Eyes for the Blind, who allows me to run by myself without worry.

My experience with track alongside experience with blindness motivated me to major at Adapted Physical Education at SUNY Brockport. During my time as an Adapted Physical Education major, I have helped professors and educators learn how to make a more accessible classroom where I and students like me can thrive and even helped Microsoft develop software for the sport of orienteering, which can be quite difficult with guide dogs as they are trained to follow specific paths.

Me and my trainer John running the marathon at 2022 Lake Placid Ironman. We are tethered at the hip so I can better follow him and the course.

In August 2021, on my birthday, I committed to compete in the 2022 Lake Placid Ironman despite having no triathalon, let alone Ironman experience. An Ironman is a special kind of triathalon consisting of a 2.4 mile swim, a 112 mile bike ride, and a marathon run. With the help of my parents and my coach, 19-time Ironman veteran John Slyer, helped me through the tough year of training required. Although it was difficult, we found ways to circumvent my blindness. For example, when running and swimming me and John use a tether at hip so I can follow him while still keeping a large enough range of motion to comfortably run. Additionally, we would use a tandem bike so John could lead while I assist with pedalling.

All that hard work paid off and on July 24th, 2022 I completed the Lake Placid Ironman just under the 17 hour cutoff. It is believed that I am the first blind person to ever completed the Lake Placid Ironman.

On top of my athletic success, my goal is to support other visually impaired athletes. I currently work as a personal coach at Camp Abilities teaching children and teens how to participate in sports while having a visual impairment. Additionally, I create content on my Youtube Channel where I teach others about how I navigate through life blind, be it in sports, college, or how I go about my day to day life with Hero.
